Why we built this

The hard part isn't knowing
how to revise. It's the doing.

We wrote a whole guide on why active recall and spaced repetition beat every other revision method. The science is settled. The problem is that building good flashcards from scratch takes hours, and most students never get round to it.

So we did it for you. Every deck covers the full exam board specification, written as proper testable questions rather than glossary definitions. Drop them into Anki, do a few minutes a day, and you're using both techniques without having to think about it.

No other tutoring company does this. It's one of the things that makes us different, and it's a resource you keep whether you ever book a session or not.

New to Anki?

Here's how it works.

Anki is a free flashcard app that schedules your revision for you. Here's the whole thing in four steps.

1

Download a deck

Pick your subject and exam board. You get a single Anki file, ready to import.

2

Import it to Anki

Open Anki, click import, done. The whole deck loads in seconds.

3

Revise daily

Anki shows you the cards due that day. You answer, you rate how well you knew it.

4

Let the algorithm work

Cards you know come back rarely. Cards you fail come back often. Until everything sticks.

Is Anki free? On desktop, web, and Android, yes, completely. The iPhone app is the one exception, with a one-off App Store fee that funds the whole free project. If you're on iPhone, you can still use Anki free in any browser at ankiweb.net.
